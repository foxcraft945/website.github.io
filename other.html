Recommended Reading

  • The Age of Reason (1796) by Thomas Paine
  • America's Four Gods: What We Say about God -- and What That Says about us by Paul Froese and Christopher Bader
  • The Believing Brain: From Ghosts and Gods to Politics and Conspiracies - How We Construct Beliefs and Reinforce Them as Truths by Michael Shermer
  • Beyond Religion: Ethics for a Whole World by H.H. Dalai Lama
  • Comforting Thoughts About Death That Have Nothing to Do with God by Greta Christina
  • Demon Haunted World: Science as a Candle in the Dark by Carl Sagan
  • The Ethics of Belief (1877) by William K. Clifford - "It is wrong always, everywhere, and for anyone, to believe anything upon insufficient evidence."
  • Faith Versus Fact: Why Science and Religion are Incompatible by Jerry A. Coyne
  • Fooled by Randomness by Nassim Nicholas Taleb
  • The Gnostic Gospels by Elaine Pagels
  • God and the Folly of Faith: The Incompatibility of Science and Religion by Victor J. Stenger
  • The Gods (1872) by Robert Green Ingersoll
  • How to Live a Good Life: A Guide to Choosing Your Personal Philosophy by Massimo Pigliucci, Skye Cleary, and Daniel Kaufman
  • Logically Fallcious: The Ultimate Collection of Over 300 Logical Fallacies by Bo Bennett
  • Mistakes Were Made (But Not by Me): Why We Justify Foolish Beliefs, Bad Decisions, and Hurtful Acts by Carol Tavris and Elliot Aronson
  • Our Political Nature: The Evolutionary Origins of What Divides Us by Avi Tuschman
  • Prometheus Bedeviled: Science and the Contradictions of Contempoary Culture by Norman Levitt
  • The Selfish Gene by Richard Dawkins
  • Sense and Goodness Without God: A Defense of Metaphysical Naturalism by Richard Carrier
  • Tetralogue: I'm Right, You're Wrong by Timothy Williamson
  • The Thinker's Guide to Fallacies: The Art of Mental Trickery by Richard Paul and Linda Elder
  • The Varieties of Scientific Experience: A Personal View of the Search for God by Carl Sagan

Web Sites

  • American Humanist Association -- Good without a god.
  • Facebook -- David Hurtubise's Facebook page: Math, Science, Philosophy, Skepticism, Humanism.
  • Fallacy Files -- Nice collection of logical fallacies and other sorts of bad reasoning.
  • The Friendly Atheist -- Excellent blog written by a (former) high school math teacher near Chicago.
  • Infidels.org -- Extensive collection of writings about various religions - "a drop of reason in a pool of confusion".
  • James Randi Educational Foundation -- Offering a (still unclaimed) million-dollar prize since 1996 to anyone who can produce evidence of paranormal abilities.
  • The Skeptic's Annotated Bible -- Includes the Koran and The Book of Mormon.
  • The Skeptic's Dictionary -- "Exploring strange beliefs, amusing deceptions, and dangerous delusions since 1994".
  • The Skeptics Society -- "Examining extraordinary claims and promoting science".
  • Snopes -- Articles on urban legends, folklore, myths, rumors, and misinformation.
  • What's The Harm? -- A site "designed to make a point about the danger of not thinking critically."
  • YourMorals.org -- "where you can learn about your own morality, ethics, and/or values, while also contributing to scientific research."
